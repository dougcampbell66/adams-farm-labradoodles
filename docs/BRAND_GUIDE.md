# Adams Farm Labradoodles — Brand Guide

## Positioning

Adams Farm Labradoodles is a Gold Paw ALAA-accredited, home-based breeder in
Greensboro, NC. The brand's job is to signal two things simultaneously,
without letting either one undercut the other:

1. **Credibility** — Gold Paw health testing, ALAA accreditation, a real
   program with standards.
2. **Warmth** — puppies raised inside the home, alongside a family, not in a
   kennel.

Most competing breeder sites lead with warmth and treat credentials as an
afterthought (a badge in the footer), or lead with credentials and read as
clinical. The differentiation opportunity is doing both without either one
feeling bolted on — trust signals presented in the same warm voice as the
rest of the site, not a separate "official" section that reads differently.

## Messaging pillars

Pulled from the brand's own existing language (Instagram bio) rather than
invented:

- ALAA Gold Paw accredited
- Raised in the home, with the family
- Excellence in health and temperament
- Well-suited to therapy and family dog roles
- Early neurological stimulation / structured socialization from birth

These five should recur across the site — hero section, an "our program"
page, and the footer trust strip — rather than being confined to one About
paragraph.

## Voice

- Warm, direct, plain-spoken. Not corporate, not cutesy.
- Confident about credentials without lecturing — state the fact, don't
  oversell it ("Gold Paw accredited" not "we're proud to have achieved the
  prestigious Gold Paw distinction").
- Never imply anything not literally true (no farm/pasture language while
  operating from a residential home in Greensboro).
- Testimonials carry the emotional warmth; the brand's own copy stays
  measured and factual.

## Color palette — finalized: "Navy & sand"

Chosen from three explored directions (sage/clay, teal/gold, navy/sand).
Navy & sand reads as the most deliberately designed of the three — a good
fit given this site may double as a portfolio piece for other ALAA/WALA
breeders considering joining the Puppy Therapy network. The tradeoff: this
direction is the least overtly "farm-warm" of the options, which is why body
copy stays on a warm, approachable sans rather than a cold grotesk — the
warmth lives in the typography and photography, not the palette.

Still **to be reconciled against the real 2026–2027 Gold Paw logo file**
once available, since accreditation logos are typically used un-recolored
and the surrounding palette should sit comfortably next to them, not clash.

## Typography

- Display: **Playfair Display** (serif, upscale, editorial) — headlines only
- Body: **Nunito Sans** — everything else; this is the deliberate warmth
  counterweight to the more formal display face and navy/sand palette
- Two weights only: 400 regular, 700 for emphasis. No heavier weights.

## Logo & accreditation marks

- Adams Farm wordmark: italic serif lockup, no icon — placeholder until a
  real logo exists (currently text-only wordmark, per the live site).
- ALAA Gold Paw badge (2026–2027): reserve a fixed-size slot in the footer
  and on an "Our accreditation" page. Do not recolor, stretch, or place text
  over the badge. Minimum clear space equal to the badge's own height on all
  sides.
- ALAA Member Breeder Number and valid-through date must appear adjacent to
  the badge (ALAA requirement, not a style choice).

## Imagery style

- Real photos only — no stock imagery standing in for "farm" or "kennel"
  scenes that don't reflect reality.
- Home environment, natural light, candid over posed where possible.
- Puppies and parents both represented — credibility work comes from parent
  photos + health info, not just puppy cuteness.
